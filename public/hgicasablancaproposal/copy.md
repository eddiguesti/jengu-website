## Section 6 — How It Goes

**Eyebrow tag**
05 — How It Goes

**Headline** (Playfair italic)
Three movements.<br>[gradient]No black box.[/gradient]

**Sub-paragraph**
You're in the room for every call that matters. We map. You decide. Then we build.

### Phase i — Map
- Kicker: Phase one
- Name: Map.
- Lead: A few weeks of remote sessions to capture how the property really runs.
- Body: Video calls with each HoD. Screen-shares of the systems they actually use. Photo and video clips from your team during the busy moments. Every workflow drawn as it happens, not as the SOP says. You see the maps as we go, so you can push back early.
- Outcome: A clean set of process maps and a shortlist of where the friction sits.

### Phase ii — Decide
- Kicker: Phase two
- Name: Decide.
- Lead: We sit down and pick the wins together.
- Body: Walk through what we found. Pick what's worth doing first — by impact, and by what you can push through without spending political capital. Some items your team owns. Some we do.
- Outcome: A prioritised plan, agreed with you, not for you.

### Phase iii — Build
- Kicker: Phase three
- Name: Build.
- Lead: The tools that lock the wins in.
- Body: Whatever the maps pointed at. WhatsApp assistant. AI-drafted reviews. Daily reports. Predictive maintenance. Built brand-safe, designed to live with your team after we leave.
- Outcome: Working tools, owned by you. No retainer. No vendor lock-in.

**Callout — eyebrow**
A note on access

**Callout — body**
We don't ask for OnQ, Honors or Connect+ access. Maps come from observation and conversations with your team. The tools live alongside the Hilton stack, not inside it. Anything that needs brand sign-off gets flagged in the map. You decide whether to push for it.
