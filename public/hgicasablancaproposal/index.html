01 — Why This

Map First.
Build Second.

Most hotels buy tools first and ask questions later. A bundle of subscriptions, nobody using them, the team back to spreadsheets. Mapping first tells you where the true pain points and bottlenecks lie and, in turn, what's worth building. Not a product list but a clear overview of where the value can best be delivered.

02 — How We Map

One Process,
Sketched Out.

A guest requests an extra towel. Five steps, two of them subtly broken.

Guest Calls reception
Reception Writes on a notepad
Reception Radios housekeeping
Lost in radio noise No record. No-one's tracking it.
Housekeeping Goes to the room when free
No SLA Could be five minutes. Could be an hour.
Guest Receives the towel

When sketched out, the broken segments are made visible. Multiply this by 50 events happening every day across the property — check-in, breakfast service, OTA bookings, complaints, room service, maintenance tickets, etc. — and you get a clear picture of where time and money are leaking. The fixes start to become apparent.

In this case the fix is

A central guest-request log (WhatsApp, app, or shared dashboard) with SLA tracking. One change makes the same friction disappear from dozens of similar workflows. That's what process mapping highlights.

03 — Some Examples

What Tends
to Come Out

Not predictions but things hotels are running right now and obtaining value from. Which ones fit HGI Sud? We'll know when we have the map.

01
WhatsApp guest assistant.
Pre-loaded with your hotel info, menu, local recommendations, FAQs. Replies in whichever language the guest writes in. Handles the easy 70%: late checkout, restaurant recommendations, breakfast hours, transfers. Anything needing PMS data hands back to your team. WhatsApp and web. No OnQ access.
02
AI-drafted review responses.
Reads the public Booking, TripAdvisor and Google pages. Drafts a reply in your voice within the hour. Your team approves in batches and posts from the normal accounts. Response time well inside the 48-hour brand standard. No Hilton integration.
03
Pre-arrival upsell flow.
Reservations drops arrivals into the tool — CSV, paste, or forwarded confirmations. It drafts personalized emails: upgrade, late checkout, breakfast, transfer. Your team approves in batches and sends from the usual account. No PMS integration required.
04
Predictive maintenance on HVAC and key kit.
Smart sensors on HVAC, boilers and lifts. Flags failures before guests do. Engineering side only, no OnQ touch. Alerts route through WhatsApp or wherever your team already works. Useful at a property with heavy A/C load.
05
Quiet prep for the HotelKey rollout.
Hilton is replacing OnQ. HGI is in the first wave. The maps make migration smoother — you already know which workflows depend on which systems and where the workarounds lie.
06
Centralizing what's scattered.
Maintenance tickets, lost and found, supplier comms, shift notes. One place everyone can see. Lives outside the Hilton stack. No integration required. Always works.

05 — How It Goes

Three movements.
No black box.

You're involved in every step that matters. We map. You decide. Then we build.

i
Phase one
Map.

A few weeks of remote sessions to capture how the property really runs.

Short interviews with floor staff, heads of department and the GM. Comparing how each level describes the same process reveals where management's view and the operational reality diverge — that's typically where the friction sits. Screen-shares of the systems people use day-to-day. Maps shared with you as we progress, so you can give feedback early.

A clean set of process maps and a shortlist of the key friction points.

ii
Phase two
Decide.

We sit down and pick the candidates together.

Walk through what we found. Pick what's best done first – by impact, and by what you can push through without pushback from superiors. Some items your team owns. Some we do.

A prioritized plan, agreed upon with you.

iii
Phase three
Build.

The tools that lock the wins in.

Whatever the maps pointed at, whether WhatsApp assistants, AI-drafted review bots, daily reports, predictive maintenance, we implement. Built brand-safe, designed to live with your team permanently.

Working tools, owned by you. No retainer. No vendor lock-in.

A note on access

We don't ask for OnQ, Honors or Connect+ access. Maps come from observation and conversations with your team. The tools live alongside the Hilton stack, not inside it. Anything that needs brand sign-off gets flagged in the map. You decide whether to push for it.
