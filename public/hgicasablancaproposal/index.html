01 — Why This

The Cleanest Way
to Find Real AI Wins

Most hotels jump straight to buying tools. The result is a stack of subscriptions that don't talk to each other. Mapping how the property actually runs first means we spot the real opportunities — the spots where AI or smart tooling would quietly take work off people's plates. Not "here's a list of products." More like "here's where you'd actually get value."

02 — Some Examples

Things We
Tend to Surface

Not a prediction list, just the kind of stuff that often turns up in a hotel like this. Won't know which ones actually apply until we look.

01

A guest messaging assistant. WhatsApp, email, web chat, in three or four languages. Picks up the easy 70% (FAQs, late-checkout requests, restaurant recs), hands the rest to your team.

02

AI-drafted review responses. Booking.com, Tripadvisor, Google. Drafts reply in your voice within an hour of a review going up. You approve in bulk.

03

Daily reports written for you. Flash, MTD, owner updates — auto-generated from the PMS instead of someone assembling them by hand every morning.

04

Spreadsheets kept up by hand. Things that should be a small dashboard or a one-line AI input. Almost always one or two of these per department.

05

Dashboards that should exist but don't. Numbers your team currently chases between three different systems every morning. Pulled into one screen.

06

Corporate RFPs taking days to answer. AI drafts a personalised response from your rate logic and past wins. You tweak and send same-day.

07

Centralising things scattered across tools. Maintenance tickets, lost & found, supplier comms, shift notes — into one place everyone can see.
