01 — Why a Process Map

See How the Property
Actually Runs

Most hotels run on a mix of brand systems, long-tenured habits and undocumented handoffs. Nobody — not even the people doing the work — has a single, true picture of how it all fits together. A process map creates that picture.

Clarity

Every step, visible

Every step, every system, every handoff, every wait state — drawn into one diagram per workflow. The friction shows up where you can finally do something about it.

Direction

A defensible, prioritised plan

Once it's mapped, the conversation about "what to do next" becomes obvious. You move from opinion-based decisions to a structured plan you can return to, present, and prove progress against.

Brand-safe

Improvements that fit Hilton

The highest-impact opportunities at any branded property almost always live around the brand systems — in the workflow, communication and service design layered on top. That's exactly what we map.

02 — Scope

Eight Workflows,
One Property

The eight workflows that touch the most guests and the most staff hours. Each becomes a swim-lane diagram showing every step, system, handoff and wait state.

● Front Office

Check-in & check-out

Arrival flow, ID and payment, key issuance, loyalty recognition, room-not-ready handling.

● Reservations

Direct, OTA & group

Channel sync, group blocks, modifications, cancellations, RFP responses, rate logic.

● Housekeeping

Room status & flow

Stayover vs check-out priority, queue rooms, deep cleans, lost & found, late checkouts.

● Garden Grille & Bar

Breakfast, F&B & in-room

Cover counts, allergens, room service, bar tabs to room, supplier ordering, charge reconciliation.

● Meetings & Events

RFP through to invoice

RFP responses, BEO distribution, AV setup, F&B coordination, post-event invoicing.

● Engineering

Tickets, PPM & OOO

Ticket logging, vendor coordination, PPM schedule, room out-of-service, brand engineering.

● Guest Communication

Pre-arrival to post-stay

Pre-arrival, in-stay channels, late-checkout requests, complaint escalation, review responses.

● Night Audit & Reporting

Close, flash & handover

Audit close, daily flash, vs-budget reporting, manager-on-duty handover, brand compliance.

If your priorities are different — for example breakfast and reservations are fine but engineering is on fire — we trim and re-weight the scope before kickoff. The price doesn't change.

Week 1 · Discover

Kickoff & stakeholder interviews

30-minute kickoff to lock scope. Then short conversations across the team — most done in 20-minute slots between shifts so the operation never pauses.

Week 2 · Map

Two days on-property + draft maps

Two days at HGI Casablanca Sud during the rhythms that matter — morning rush, breakfast service, afternoon arrivals, one full night audit. Drafts of the maps mid-week for you to react to.

Week 3 · Score & Deliver

Bottleneck analysis & readout

Each step in each map is reviewed. Improvements ranked by impact and ease. Final readout meeting (60 min, in-person or Teams) with you and whoever you want in the room.
