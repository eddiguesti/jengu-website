01 — Why Start Here

Map First,
Then Automate

Most hotels jump straight to buying tools. The result is a stack of subscriptions that don't talk to each other and staff who go back to manual workarounds. Mapping first means every Dirham you spend later lands on the highest-impact bottleneck.

Find what's actually broken

Reception saying "we don't have time" is rarely about reception. It's a downstream symptom of a missed handoff with housekeeping, an OTA sync delay, or a Honors-tier flag that never made it into the morning brief. Mapping makes the real cause visible.

A baseline you can prove gains against

If you don't know how long check-in takes today, you can't prove a kiosk saved 4 minutes per guest. The map becomes the before-photo. Six months later, the after-photo writes itself.

Lower-risk than buying tools blind

A 3-week diagnostic is far cheaper than rolling out a guest messaging platform that 30% of staff bypass. We hand you a ranked roadmap so the first thing you buy is the thing with the clearest ROI.

"You can't optimise what you can't see. Process mapping turns a hundred individual frustrations into one clear picture of where the property is bleeding hours."

— Jengu engagement principle

Stakeholder interviews — 15-20 minute sit-downs with the GM, FOM, Housekeeping Manager, F&B Manager, Reservations lead, Chief Engineer, and 2-3 line staff per department. Where the pain actually lives.
Shadow observation — two days on-property, watching the actual flow during morning rush, breakfast service, mid-afternoon arrivals, and the night audit. No clipboards out, no theatre.
Swim-lane process maps — every step, every system (OnQ, channel manager, Hilton Honors, F&B POS, BEO tool), every handoff and every wait state. Industry-standard BPMN notation so anyone can read it.
Bottleneck & waste analysis — we mark the eight Lean wastes (waiting, defects, over-processing, etc.) directly on the maps. Now the painful steps are coloured red and the wins are obvious.
Opportunity scoring — every fix gets two scores: Impact (hours saved/week, guest-experience uplift, revenue captured) and Ease (build effort, change-management cost, risk). Plotted on a 2×2 — quick wins are top-right.
