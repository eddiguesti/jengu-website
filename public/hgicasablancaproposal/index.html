03 — Scope

Eight Workflows,
One Property

The eight workflows that touch the most guests and the most staff hours. Each becomes a swim-lane map showing every step, every system, every handoff, every wait state. The questions below are what we'd investigate — actual findings depend entirely on what we observe at HGI Casablanca Sud.

● Front Office

Check-in & check-out

Arrival flow, ID/passport scan, payment authorisation, key issuance, loyalty-tier recognition, room-not-ready handling.

Question we'd ask: how is loyalty tier surfaced to reception, and could pre-arrival flagging save the recognition step at the desk?
● Reservations

Direct, OTA & group flow

Channel manager sync, group blocks, modifications, cancellations, no-show enforcement, RFP response time, rate logic.

Question we'd ask: what's the lag between an OTA booking and PMS visibility, and is it ever the cause of a walked guest?
● Housekeeping

Room status & flow

Stayover vs check-out priority, queue rooms, deep-clean rotation, lost & found, minibar posting, late-checkout coordination.

Question we'd ask: how does a room go from physically ready to PMS-ready — and where does that handoff live today?
● Garden Grille & Bar

Breakfast, F&B & in-room

Cover counts, allergen handling, room service tickets, bar tabs to room, supplier ordering, end-of-shift cash-up, charge-to-room reconciliation.

Question we'd ask: how do allergens travel from guest to kitchen, and how often do charges-to-room get disputed at checkout?
● Meetings & Events

RFP through to invoice

RFP responses, BEO distribution, AV setup, F&B coordination, post-event invoicing, corporate-rate enforcement, master account balancing.

Question we'd ask: from event close to invoice raised, how many days pass — and where does the cash sit in the meantime?
● Engineering

Tickets, PPM & OOO

Ticket logging, vendor coordination, PPM schedule, room out-of-service flow, energy/HVAC routine checks, brand engineering compliance.

Question we'd ask: what % of maintenance tickets originate from guest complaints versus being caught proactively?
● Guest Communication

Pre-arrival to post-stay

Pre-arrival messaging, in-stay channels, late-checkout requests, complaint escalation, post-stay survey, online review responses.

Question we'd ask: how does a guest currently reach the right person mid-stay, and how fast does a negative review get a property response?
● Night Audit & Reporting

Close, flash & handover

Night audit close time, daily flash, MTD/YTD vs budget, REVPAR & ADR review, manager-on-duty handover, brand compliance reports.

Question we'd ask: when does the morning meeting actually start having data to discuss, versus the time it's scheduled?

If your priorities are different — say breakfast and reservations are fine but engineering is on fire — we trim and re-weight the scope before kickoff. The price doesn't change.

Week 1 · Discover

Kickoff & stakeholder interviews

30-minute kickoff with you to lock scope. Then 8–12 stakeholder conversations across the team (FOM, Housekeeping, F&B, Reservations, Sales, Chief Engineer, plus 2–3 line staff per department). Most done in 20-min slots between shifts so the operation never pauses.

Week 2 · Map

Two days on-property + draft maps

Two days at HGI Casablanca Sud during the rhythms that matter — morning rush, breakfast service, afternoon arrivals, one full night audit. Back at the desk we draft the eight swim-lane maps. You see drafts mid-week to react to before we move on.

Week 3 · Score & Deliver

Bottleneck analysis, scoring, executive readout

Each step in each map is marked against the eight Lean wastes. Each improvement is scored on Impact × Ease and plotted. Quick-wins playbook drafted. Final readout meeting (60 min, in-person or Teams) with you and whoever else you want in the room.

After Week 3 · Optional

Implementation support — your call, separate quote

If you want us to build the top opportunities (the AI email triage, the housekeeping app, the reservations sync, whatever surfaces) we quote each as a separate fixed-fee project after the readout. No retainer, no pressure to continue.
