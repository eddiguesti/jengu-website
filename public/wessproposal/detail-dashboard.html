Central Operations
Dashboard

One unified view of the entire WESS group. Live occupancy, revenue by channel, rate parity status, outstanding communications, purchasing alerts — all in one place. The product that makes a centralised head office possible.

Peter's Vision

Centralised Head Office. One View.

The goal: Peter is building a centralised head office to manage the growing WESS portfolio. His whole objective is one unified view of the business — not logging into five different systems, not waiting for property managers to email reports, not piecing together the picture from fragmented data. One screen, every number that matters, updated in real time.

From Fragmentation to Clarity

Today, understanding the state of the business requires checking Opera for occupancy, Sage for financials, email for TO communications, Material Control for purchasing, and spreadsheets for everything else. The dashboard consolidates all of this into a single, live view — pulling data from every system into one interface.

What It Shows

Everything That Matters

Live Occupancy

Real-time occupancy across all WESS properties. Today's occupancy, this week's forecast, next month's projection. Colour-coded by performance against target. At a glance, you know which properties need attention.

Bookings by Channel

How many bookings came through Booking.com vs Expedia vs direct vs tour operators this week? This month? This quarter? See channel mix shifting in real time and spot trends early.

Rate Parity Status

Are your rates the same across all channels right now? The dashboard shows parity status at a glance — green for matched, red for violations. Click through to see which channel is off and by how much.

TO Communications

Outstanding tour operator emails awaiting response. How many are in queue? How long has the oldest been waiting? Which need human review? The email agent's status at a glance.

Purchasing Status

Open purchase orders, pending approvals, delivery exceptions, and stock alerts across all properties. The head office sees every outlet's purchasing activity without logging into the purchasing system.

Revenue by Outlet

How is each restaurant, bar, spa, and rooms division performing? Daily revenue by outlet, compared to budget and last year. F&B performance at a glance across the entire portfolio.

Morning Briefing Automation

Peter's Notion, but for the Whole Group

Peter already has a Notion setup that tells him his day at 9 o'clock in the morning. We build a proper version of this for the entire WESS operation. At 9am, the dashboard compiles an automated morning briefing: yesterday's occupancy and revenue across all properties, today's arrivals and VIP guests, outstanding items requiring attention, purchasing approvals waiting, and TO emails that need human review. Delivered to Notion, email, or the dashboard itself — Peter's choice.

Platform Options

Notion or Standalone

Option A: Built into Notion

Peter already lives in Notion. We can build the dashboard and morning briefing directly within his existing Notion workspace — using Notion databases, automated pages, and API integrations. The advantage: zero new tools to learn. The data flows into where Peter already works.

Option B: Standalone Web App

A purpose-built dashboard application accessible from any browser. More visual flexibility — live charts, interactive maps, drill-down analytics. Shareable with the wider team without needing Notion access. Can still push the morning briefing to Notion.

Our recommendation: Start with Notion integration for the morning briefing (fast to deliver, Peter already uses it) and build the standalone dashboard in parallel. Peter gets immediate value from the briefing while the full dashboard takes shape. Both can coexist — Notion for Peter's daily workflow, standalone for the operations team and GMs.

What We Need from the Process Map

To Build the Right Dashboard

Process Map Required

A dashboard is only as good as the data it shows and the decisions it enables. We need to understand what Peter and the team actually need to see to run the business effectively.

What KPIs matter? — Occupancy, ADR, RevPAR, GOP — which metrics does Peter look at first? What does the board report require? What do the GMs need?
What data sources exist? — Opera, Sage, Symphony POS, the purchasing system, the channel manager — which systems hold the data? How is it currently accessed?
What decisions are made from this data? — Understanding what actions follow from the numbers helps us prioritise what's on the dashboard vs. what's a drill-down report.
Who else needs access? — Is this Peter-only? Does each GM need a property-specific view? Does the finance team need their own slice? The access model determines the architecture.
Peter's current Notion setup — What does the existing Notion morning briefing look like? What does he like about it? What's missing? We build on what works.

"9 o'clock in the morning, my Notion is telling me PETER today..." — Imagine that, but for every property, every department, every number that matters. Built into Notion or as a standalone web app.
